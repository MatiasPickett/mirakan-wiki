### Appearance
It has the appearance of a semi-translucent cristal with shades that can vary from deep purple to cyan, depending on the potency and method of production.
### Effects
Purpur consumption, generally done by letting the crystal dissolve on the tongue has the following effects:
- Euphoria
- Hallucinations
- Spontaneous wild magic manifestation

These effects are much more mild when affecting elves, possibly due to their magical nature. In their culture, it is used as a recreational drug for celebrations and other occasions.
### History
Purpur was discovered by elven alchemists several centuries ago and only recently has been introduced in Mirakas.
### Variants
- **Synthetic Purpur** is synthesized by knowledgable wizards and alchemists with the correct equipment and time, and produces a product with a slight cyan shade and a touch of purple. Its effects are not as intense but still highly addictive.

- **Organic Purpur** must be grown by living beings afflicted with purpur plague, a condition in which due to the consumption of large quantities of the drug, the body begins synthesizing its own. Clumps of the cristal begin forming behind the eyes and underneath the nails, before eventually bursting forth through the skin, where they can then be collected and sold. This method of Purpur farming is seen as extremely inhumane and normally avoided however it does produce a higher potency purpur with a deeper purple color and stronger effects.
